Internal PC Components


  • Motherboards
    1. Backbone of the computer.
    2. Interconnects computer components.

  • CPU
    1. The brain of the computer.
    2. Most processing is done by the CPU.

  • Cooling Systems
    1. Dissipates the heat generated by computer components.

  • Memory (ROM and RAM)
    1. RAM: Stores data temporarily, aiding processing.
    2. ROM: Stores data permanently; often storing firmware and low level programs.

  • Adapter Cards and Expansion Slots
    1. Adapter Cards extend computer functionality.
    2. Adapter Cards connect to the motherboard through Expansion Slots.

  • Storage Devices
    1. Designed to permanently store user data, user applications and the Operating System.
    2. Can be internal or external to the computer.

  • Video Ports
    1. Connects a video system to an external display device such as a monitor or projector.
    2. Video systems are often designed as an adapter card.

  • General Ports
    1. RAM: Stores data temporarily, aiding processing.
    2. Provide connectivity between the motherboard and various external devices such as printers, external storage and video cameras.

External Ports and Cables


  • Video Ports and Related Cables
    1. A few different standards govern video traffic between the computer and external video devices.
    2. HDMI and displayPort are examples of video ports that require a specific cable to operate.

  • Other Ports and Related Cables
    1. Motherboards have a number of other ports used for device connectivity; USB is a common example.

  • Adapters and Converters
    1. Adapters and converters can be a solution if a motherboard does not have the proper port to connect to a device.
    2. Adapters do not usually process the signal; they simply redirect it to another pin.
    3. Converters are more likely to process and transform the signal, converting it to be accepted by an existing port.

    2. ROM: Stores data permanently; often storing firmware and low level programs.
