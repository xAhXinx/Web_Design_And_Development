Desktop Tutorial

How to build up your own desktop?

Below will teach you how to build ur own desktop one by one.

  1. Install the power supply and plug it into the outlet. Triple check it is on the OFF position, or connect it to a surge protector (also turned off).
  2. Ground yourself to prevent frying any components. Here’s a guide, but it basically involves touching an unpainted metal surface on the case or power supply NOT other components). Many power supplies comes with unpainted metal screws; I like using those once installed.
  3. Screw in your motherboard. Cases usually come with standoffs to lift your motherboard a bit from the case. Make sure you use them.
  4. Install the CPU. Make sure it’s in the right orientation, and pull the lever down all the way.
  5. Install the cooler. Follow the guide for your particular unit, and make sure to apply thermal paste if it doesn’t already have it, or else you’ll fry your CPU.
  6. Insert your RAM into the slots. Make sure it’s inserted properly, such that the little clips at the sides snap all the way in.
  7. Mount your storage onto your PC. This varies depending your case and storage type. An M.2 SSD plugs directly into your motherboard, while most others can attached to your case via screws or special slots. You’ll attach them to your motherboard via a cable later.
  8. Install your graphics card into the PCI-E slot. Make sure it’s all the way in; it uses a clip similar to the RAM.
